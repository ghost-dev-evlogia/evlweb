## Part 1 — Voice analysis (what makes PostHog's copy work)

**Tone:** confident, plain-spoken, engineer-to-engineer. Never corporate. Jokes are dry and land in the
*margins* (footers, cookie banners, fine print) so the main pitch stays clear. It sells to builders by
sounding like a builder, and it earns trust by being self-deprecating rather than boastful.

**Signature moves to steal:**
- **Punchy, declarative headlines.** "The new way to build products." No adjectival throat-clearing.
- **One clear value claim, stated flatly.** "PostHog is the only platform that acts like a co-pilot for
  you (and your AI agents) to do it all – autonomously."
- **Plain-English identity lines.** "PostHog is a single platform for people who build things." /
  "We're here to help product engineers build successful products."
- **Jokes in the fine print, not the headline.** The brand personality lives in microcopy:
  - *"PostHog is a web product and cannot be installed by CD. We did once send some customers a floppy
    disk but it was a Rickroll."*
  - *"Hurry: 1882 companies signed up today. Act now and get $0 off your first order."* (parodies
    fake-scarcity marketing while using it)
  - Cookie banner: *"PostHog.com doesn't use third-party cookies, only a single in-house cookie. No data
    is sent to a third party. (Ursula von der Leyen would be so proud.)"*
- **Anti-polish grammar as a wink.** "There are other dev tool companies, but they not like us." (the
  deliberate grammatical break signals in-group, not-a-marketing-deck)
- **Product names as plain nouns.** Web Analytics, Session Replay, Funnels, Heatmaps, User Paths — no
  invented jargon.
- **Radical pricing transparency** as a brand stance (see the pricing page raw capture below).

**Lesson for Evlogia's farm voice:** keep the *pitch* rigorous and literal (engineer-first claims,
verifiable numbers), and let the *farm world* + the *microcopy* carry the personality — jokes in the
tooltips, empty states, and fine print, not in the headline claims. This is exactly "whimsy in the
world, rigor in the words."

## Who we're looking for

We're hiring for a range of roles here. What you end up working on will depend on your exact skillset and preferences, so choose your own adventure.

In general we seek Product Engineers who are:

-

Enthusiastic drivers. We need proactive people that can fully own projects and get them done, and know to get help when needed. This is what being a product engineer is all about. "Are we there yet?" is the wrong question.

-

Optimistic problem solvers. Things get hard here sometimes – whether it's scaling, shipping complex products, handling a stream of support requests, or trying to ship something that touches multiple teams. We need people who won't get disheartened, and will collaborate, iterate, and ship their way out of anything.

-

Grown ups. We’re an international bunch of weirdos, but one thing unites us: everyone is kind, considerate, and professional towards each other. This isn't about age or experience, it's about being low-ego, flexible, and respectful.

-

Genuine builders. PostHog is full of people who just love building stuff, people who would still be building software even if there wasn't a paycheck at the end. If this sounds like you, we should talk.
